Luftig

A smart Air Conditioner.

For your smart home.

This site is a part of the Capstone X IKEA project
of group 8 in the Chalmers University course,
Digitalization and AI in practice (TEK830).
The "Luftig" project is a software prototype designed
to be used inside of Air Conditioners with the purpose
of minimizing energy consumption and in turn
decrease utility costs for US households.

How the project upholds the three design thinking principles.

Desirability

The product focuses on reducing utility costs in US homes by reducing the electricity consumption of their air conditioning unit. With air conditioners already being present in about 90% of US households, their power consumption accounts for a big chunk of each such household's electrical bill, and about 27% of US households have been reported to struggle to pay such bills. We firmly believe that this product will be appreciated by many potential customers looking to get a better grasp of their energy consumption, as well as enabling a smart solution for a more climate conscious way of cooling their home. And since the ac will learn to keep up a comfortable climate at home, best fitting the user, the customers will be paying less for the same cool home, cool!

Feasability

The product idea is not too different compared to common air conditioners across the market, with the exception of smart firmware linked to the IKEA smart home app which allows the special features. The project would obviously require product design and mechanical engineering to deploy an actual product, but also some way of processing and collecting the data needed for our smart functions to work properly, this is however something that would ideally be solved through our product's connection with the IKEA smart home app which already has good and secure solutions to those needs. Other than that the product would also need an actual tie in with either a brand of ACs or ideally a solution from IKEA themselves.

Viability

The product follows IKEAs current business model by focusing on reducing energy consumption and thus in turn reducing prices in the long run for the common household owners. The act of reducing energy consumption also follows IKEAs sustainability goals by reducing the AC:s impact on the climate and also helping the customers to reduce their monthly electricity bill.
